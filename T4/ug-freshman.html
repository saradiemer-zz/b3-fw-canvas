We encourage you to visit both campuses, especially if your areas of interest include majors located downtown: Education, Social Work, Business Administration, Computer Science, Criminal Justice and Communication.

Ideal itineraries for visiting both campuses are:

  • 10am Lake Shore visit (10am-12pm) followed by 2:15pm Water Tower Tour (2:15pm-3:00pm)
  • 12pm Water Tower tour (12pm-12:45pm) followed by Lake Shore visit (2pm-4pm)

Lake Shore Campus

Come learn more about Loyola on the universityâ€™s beautiful Lake Shore Campus. Your visit will include a tour of the campus with a current student and an information presentation highlighting the university and the admission and financial aid process.

  • Location: The Undergraduate Admission Office is located in the Sullivan Center for Student Services
  • Days/times available: Weekday visits are offered at 10am and 2pm each day. On busy weekdays we also offer a noon session. Saturday visits are offered at 9:30am and 11am during the school year and 10am during the summer. Please allow 2 hours for a full visit of campus.

Water Tower Campus

With a current Loyola student as your guide, visit academic facilities, the Terry Student Center, and more at the Water Tower Campus. Unfortunately, we cannot offer a view of a residence hall room on this tour.

  • Location: Tours depart from the Undergraduate Admission Office on the second floor in the Schreiber Center.
  • Days/times available: Monday-Friday at 12pm and 2:15pm

During the fall and spring semester, when classes are in session, Loyola shuttle passes are available in the Undergraduate Admission Office. If the shuttle is not running, we are happy to provide driving or public transportation directions.

Loyola strives for its campus to be accessible to all individuals. If you need disability-related accommodations, please contact us at visitloyola@luc.edu.

Deadlines
August

Free online applications available for Spring and Fall 2018 beginning in August. Loyola reviews applications on a rolling admission basis throughout the year.

December 1

Priority deadline to submit all application materials for academic scholarship consideration

Early February

Application deadline for additional Loyola scholarships

March 1

Priority filing deadline for the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA). Admitted freshmen are encouraged to submit FAFSA information before this date in order to receive all their need-based financial aid information before the May 1 decision deadline.

March 15

Application deadline for Interdisciplinary Honors and Business Honors programs.

May 1

Enrollment deposit date for freshmen applicants to reserve a place in the class. The $500 deposit is required for all freshmen. Deposit refunds will not be granted after this date.
