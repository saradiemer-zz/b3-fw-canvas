Students don’t have to get their hands dirty to help with Loyola’s commitment to sustainability, but we’re not sure why you wouldn’t want to.

Our biodiesel lab is not just an educational tool. It’s a student-run, certified-green business that produces and sells fuel, soap, and much more.

During summer and fall, students help manage a weekly farmers market and sell campus-grown produce right outside the Loyola ‘L’ stop.

Whether it’s Earth Day or just because, our students often use their green thumbs to lend other Chicago communities a hand.

With 98 acres of greenery, Loyola's Retreat and Ecology Campus offers space for both conservation and adventure.

Loyola has a lot more to offer than just a greenhouse—though at 3,1000 square feet, our Ecodome does deserve a small shout-out.
