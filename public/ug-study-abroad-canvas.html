See the world.
Change the world.

Study abroad and you'll broaden your horizons. Your new world view means you'll grow as a person as you explore new cultures, make a difference and create memories for a lifetime.

Rome

The John Felice Rome Center (JFRC) is a fully-equipped American campus near both Vatican City and Rome's historic center. Founded in 1962, the JFRC serves students of any major seeking a new perspective about their place in a rapidly-changing world.

Vietnam

Loyola’s Vietnam Center in Ho Chi Minh City, where you can explore an emerging region full of life and adventure. Students will be immersed in beauty and culture of Southeast Asia through service learning and academic excursions.

At the Vietnam Center, experience the country’s sights, sounds, weather, food, culture, people, lights, and, well, everything.

With optional short trips, students can travel to either beautiful or culturally significant sites. Most of the time, it’ll be both.

Even with course outlines and trip itineraries, there’s still no way for us to prepare you for every highlight or unexpected new friend.

With programs across the globe, Loyola lets you choose the experience that fits your passions and your education.

Every semester, students make their home at the John Felice Rome Center, an old convent nestled among orange trees.

Students won’t get the traditional tourist experience, and they definitely won’t spend the entire trip stuck on a tour bus.
